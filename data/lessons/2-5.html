Vocabulary

📚 Key Terms
Substitution — an algebraic method where one equation is solved for one variable, then that expression is substituted into the other equation.
Elimination — an algebraic method that uses addition or subtraction to eliminate one variable.

Substitution Method

🔢 Steps
  1. Step 1: Solve at least one equation for one variable.
  2. Step 2: Substitute that expression into the other equation and solve.
  3. Step 3: Substitute back to find the other variable. Write as an ordered pair.

Step 1 — Solve for $x$ in Equation 2:

$x + 2y = -12$Equation 2
$x = -2y - 12$Subtract $2y$

Step 2 — Substitute into Equation 1:

$8(-2y-12) - 3y = -1$Substitute
$-16y - 96 - 3y = -1$Distribute
$-19y = 95$Add 96, simplify
$y = -5$Divide by $-19$

Step 3 — Solve for $x$:

$x + 2(-5) = -12$Substitute $y = -5$
$x = -2$Simplify
Solution: $(-2,\ -5)$

Equation 2 is already solved for $y$. Substitute $2x - 11$ for $y$:

$-5x + 2.5(2x-11) = -15$Substitute
$-5x + 5x - 27.5 = -15$Distribute
$-27.5 = -15$Variable eliminates
⚠️ $-27.5 = -15$ is false → the system has no solution. The lines are parallel.
No Solution

Ms. Washington needs 300 mL of a 5% HCl solution by mixing 3.5% ($x$ mL) and 7% ($y$ mL).

$x + y = 300$Total volume
$0.035x + 0.07y = 15$Total acid

Solve Equation 1 for $x$: $x = -y + 300$. Substitute:

$0.035(-y+300) + 0.07y = 15$Substitute
$0.035y = 4.5$Simplify
$y \approx 128.57$Divide by 0.035
$x \approx 171.43$Substitute back
≈171.43 mL of 3.5% solution and ≈128.57 mL of 7% solution

Elimination Method

🔢 Steps
  1. Step 1: Multiply one or both equations so that a variable's coefficients are opposites.
  2. Step 2: Add or subtract the equations to eliminate that variable. Solve.
  3. Step 3: Substitute back to find the other variable. Write as an ordered pair.

Multiply Equation 2 by $-1$ to create opposite $y$-terms:

$-4x - 9y = -23 \;\to\; 4x + 9y = 23$$\times(-1)$

Add Equation 1 and the new Equation 2:

$(-2x - 9y) + (4x + 9y) = -25 + 23$Add
$2x = -2 \;\to\; x = -1$Solve
$-4(-1) - 9y = -23 \;\to\; y = 3$Substitute $x = -1$
Solution: $(-1,\ 3)$

Multiply Equation 1 by 3 and Equation 2 by 2 to create matching $x$-terms:

Eq 1 $\times 3$: $\;6x + 15y = 3$
Eq 2 $\times 2$: $\;6x - 8y = -20$

Subtract Equation 2 × 2 from Equation 1 × 3:

$23y = 23 \;\to\; y = 1$Subtract, $x$ cancels
$2x + 5(1) = 1 \;\to\; x = -2$Substitute $y = 1$
Solution: $(-2,\ 1)$

Multiply Equation 2 by $-3$:

$-18x - 21y = -6$Eq 2 $\times(-3)$

Add the equations:

$(18x+21y) + (-18x-21y) = 14 + (-6)$
$0 = 8$Both variables eliminate
⚠️ $0 = 8$ is false → the system has no solution. The lines are parallel.

Special Cases

⚠️ What to Watch For
  • No solution: variables eliminate and you get a false statement (e.g. $0 = 8$). Lines are parallel — same slope, different $y$-intercept.
  • Infinitely many solutions: variables eliminate and you get a true statement (e.g. $0 = 0$). The two equations describe the same line.
