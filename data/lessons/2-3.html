Core Concepts

📐 Standard Form

Any linear equation can be written in standard form:

Standard Form $Ax + By = C$, where $A \ge 0$, $A$ and $B$ are not both zero, and $A$, $B$, $C$ are integers with a GCF of 1.

To convert: move the $x$- and $y$-terms to one side, then clear fractions by multiplying every term by the LCD — making sure $A$ ends up non-negative.

📈 Slope-Intercept Form

The most common way to write a linear equation:

Slope-Intercept Form $y = mx + b$, where $m$ is the slope (rise/run) and $b$ is the $y$-intercept (value of $y$ when $x = 0$).

To convert: isolate $y$ — move the $x$-term to the other side, then divide every term by the coefficient of $y$.

📌 Point-Slope Form

Useful when you know the slope and one point, or two points:

Point-Slope Form $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$, where $m$ is the slope and $(x_1, y_1)$ is any known point on the line.

Given two points: first find slope with $m = \dfrac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$, then substitute the slope and either point.

🌍 Interpreting Real-World Equations

In a real-world model $y = mx + b$:

  • Slope $m$ — the rate of change: how much $y$ changes per unit of $x$.
  • $y$-intercept $b$ — the starting value: the value of $y$ when $x = 0$.

Converting Between Forms

🔄 Writing an Equation from Two Points
1
Find the slope

Use $m = \dfrac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$ with the two given points.

2
Substitute into point-slope form

Plug the slope and the coordinates of either point into $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$.

3
Simplify if needed

Convert to slope-intercept or standard form as required by the problem.

Example 1
Standard Form — Write $y = \frac{2}{5}x + 14$ in standard form. Identify $A$, $B$, $C$.
▼
1
Move the x-term to the left
$y = \frac{2}{5}x + 14$
$-\frac{2}{5}x + y = 14$ ← subtract $\frac{2}{5}x$ from both sides
2
Clear fractions and make A positive
$-2x + 5y = 70$ ← multiply every term by $-5$
$2x - 5y = -70$  →  $A = 2$, $B = -5$, $C = -70$
✓ Check: Write $2y = 10x - 16$ in standard form
Rearrange: $10x - 2y = 16$ → divide by 2: $5x - y = 8$
So $A = 5$, $B = -1$, $C = 8$. ✓
Example 2
Slope-Intercept Form — Convert $12x - 4y = 24$. Identify $m$ and $b$.
▼
1
Isolate y
$12x - 4y = 24$
$-4y = -12x + 24$ ← subtract $12x$
$y = 3x - 6$ ← divide by $-4$
$y = 3x - 6$  →  $m = 3$, $b = -6$
✓ Check: Convert $4x = -2y + 22$
$2y = -4x + 22$ → $y = -2x + 11$ ✓

Point-Slope Form & Real-World Models

Example 3
Real World — Interpret $3246x - 2y = -152{,}722$ (shoe sales)
▼
1
Convert to slope-intercept form
$3246x - 2y = -152{,}722$
$-2y = -3246x - 152{,}722$ ← subtract $3246x$
$y = 1623x + 76{,}361$ ← divide by $-2$
2
Interpret slope and y-intercept
📊 Interpretation
Slope 1623: sales increased by $1623 million per year.
$b = 76{,}361$: in year $x = 0$ (2010), sales were $76,361 million.
Example 4
Building a Model — Smartphones: 1.31B users in 2013, 2.38B in 2017. Estimate 2025.
▼
1
Define variables and find slope
Let $x$ = years after 2013, $y$ = billions of users
Points: $(0,\ 1.31)$ and $(4,\ 2.38)$
$m = \dfrac{2.38 - 1.31}{4 - 0} = \dfrac{1.07}{4} = 0.2675 \approx 0.27$
2
Write the equation (b = y-intercept = 1.31)
$y = 0.27x + 1.31$
3
Estimate for 2025 (x = 12)
$y = 0.27(12) + 1.31 = 3.24 + 1.31 = 4.55$
About 4.55 billion users in 2025
⚠️ Watch Out!

This model assumes a constant rate of increase — real growth may slow as it approaches the world population.

Example 5
Point-Slope — Given slope $m = 11$ and point $(3,\ -5)$
▼
1
Substitute into point-slope form
$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$
$y - (-5) = 11(x - 3)$
$y + 5 = 11(x - 3)$
$y + 5 = 11(x - 3)$
✓ Check: Write the equation through $(13,\ -5)$ with slope $4.5$
$y - (-5) = 4.5(x - 13)$ → $y + 5 = 4.5(x - 13)$ ✓
Example 6
Two Points — Find point-slope form through $(1,\ 1)$ and $(7,\ 13)$
▼
1
Find the slope
$m = \dfrac{13 - 1}{7 - 1} = \dfrac{12}{6} = 2$
2
Either point works — both forms are equivalent
Using $(1, 1)$
$y - 1 = 2(x - 1)$
Using $(7, 13)$
$y - 13 = 2(x - 7)$
$y - 1 = 2(x - 1)$  (or equivalently $y - 13 = 2(x - 7)$)
✓ Check: Line through $(-1, 1)$ and $(-2, 13)$
$m = \dfrac{13-1}{-2-(-1)} = \dfrac{12}{-1} = -12$
Valid forms: $y - 1 = -12(x + 1)$ or $y - 13 = -12(x + 2)$ ✓
Example 7
Architecture — Tower of Pisa lean: $1.4\text{ m}$ in 1350, $5.4\text{ m}$ in 1993
▼
1
Set up: x = years after 1178
Points: $(172,\ 1.4)$ and $(815,\ 5.4)$  (1350 and 1993 are 172 and 815 yrs after 1178)
$m = \dfrac{5.4 - 1.4}{815 - 172} = \dfrac{4}{643} \approx 0.006$ m/year
2
Write point-slope equation
$y - 1.4 = 0.006(x - 172)$
$y - 1.4 = 0.006(x - 172)$
⚠️ Watch Out!

Point-slope form only approximates the trend over the given interval — it assumes a constant rate, which may not hold for all years.

🔑 Key Reminder: Which Form to Use?

Standard form — when asked for integer coefficients or comparing two variables.  |  Slope-intercept — when the slope and y-intercept are needed.  |  Point-slope — when given a point and a slope, or two points.
