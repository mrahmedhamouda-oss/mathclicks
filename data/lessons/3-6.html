Key Concepts

🔑 The Quadratic Formula

For any quadratic equation ax² + bx + c = 0 where a ≠ 0:

x = (−b ± √(b² − 4ac)) / (2a)

This formula always works — even when factoring or completing the square would be difficult.

🔑 The Discriminant

The expression under the radical, b² − 4ac, is called the discriminant. It tells you the number and type of roots without solving the equation.

Discriminant ValueNumber & Type of RootsGraph Behavior
b²−4ac > 0 (perfect square)2 real, rational roots REALParabola crosses x-axis at 2 points
b²−4ac > 0 (not perfect square)2 real, irrational roots REALParabola crosses x-axis at 2 points
b²−4ac = 01 real, rational root (double) REALParabola touches x-axis at vertex
b²−4ac < 02 complex roots COMPLEXParabola does NOT cross x-axis

🔑 Steps for Using the Quadratic Formula

  1. Write the equation in standard form: ax² + bx + c = 0
  2. Identify a, b, and c
  3. Substitute into the formula: x = (−b ± √(b²−4ac)) / 2a
  4. Simplify the discriminant under the radical
  5. Simplify to get exact or approximate solutions

= (−124.4 ± √18,163.36) / (−32)Add under radical
≈ (−124.4 ± 134.77) / (−32)√18163.36 ≈ 134.77
t ≈ 8.1 seconds or t ≈ −0.33 secondsSolve both values
