Key Concepts

🔑 What Is a Polynomial?

A polynomial is a monomial or the sum of monomials. Each term must have a non-negative integer exponent.

✓ Polynomial: 5x³ − 2x + 7
✓ Polynomial: 3a⁴b + 9 (degree = 5)
✗ NOT polynomial: x⁻² + 3 (negative exponent)
✗ NOT polynomial: ∛x − 1 (fractional exponent 1/3)

Binomial = sum of 2 monomials    Trinomial = sum of 3 monomials

ℹ Closure Property: The sum, difference, or product of two polynomials is always a polynomial. The set of polynomials is closed under +, −, and ×.

🔑 Adding & Subtracting Polynomials

Combine like terms — terms with the same variable raised to the same power.

Method 1: Horizontal

(3x² − 2x + 1) + (x² + 5x − 3)
= (3x²+x²) + (−2x+5x) + (1−3)
= 4x² + 3x − 2

Method 2: Vertical (align)

  3x² − 2x + 1
(+) x² + 5x − 3
──────────────
  4x² + 3x − 2
⚠ Subtracting: Distribute the negative sign to ALL terms of the subtracted polynomial before combining. (a − b) = a + (−b)

🔑 Multiplying Polynomials

Use the Distributive Property: multiply each term in one polynomial by each term in the other.

ℹ FOIL Method (for two binomials only):
(a + b)(c + d) = First + Outer + Inner + Last
(2x + 4)(x − 3)
First
2x · x = 2x²
Outer
2x · (−3) = −6x
Inner
4 · x = 4x
Last
4 · (−3) = −12
= 2x² − 6x + 4x − 12 = 2x² − 2x − 12

For polynomials with more than 2 terms, use the distributive property repeated times.
